Things To Do


The 9E Ranch

is a 320 acre ranch with ponds, Piney Creek, Loblolly Pines, oaks , hiking trails and gazebos with overlook of the best sunsets in Texas. Guests enjoy stargazing, hiking, forest bathing, birding, petting the miniature donkey, horses and sitting in rocking chairs relaxing. The 9E Ranch Cabins was awarded "Best for Stargazing" the first year open.



Shoppers will find

Smithville and Bastrop fun as well as excellent places to eat. Round Top, just 45 minutes away is an antiquer's paradise. Both Bastrop and Smithville are great small Texas towns with good food, historical homes, art Lost Pines Art Center, plays Bastrop Opera House and interestingBastrop Museum and Visitors Center. Smithville is proud of Smithville Veterans Memeorial Park, Train Museum and the location of films including "Hope Floats" , " Tree of Life" and "Bernie".



Golfers

stay at the 9E Ranch and play at all three golf courses in the area. ColoVista Golf Course course winds through beautiful vistas just east of Bastrop. Pine Forest Golf , an exquisite 18-hole course, carved out of the rolling hills of the Lost Pines Forest and has delighted golf enthusiasts for years with its blend of dramatic elevation changes, water hazards, sand traps and rolling fairways. Wolf Dancer Golf Club with it's 18 holes is a few miles west of Bastrop with spectacular and varied scenery, rolling prairie, wooded ridgeline and the Colorado River valley. The course occupies 150 of the 405 acre Hyatt Regency Lost Pines Hotel & Resort.



Bastrop State Park (tpwd.texas.gov)

The park provides opportunities for picnicking, canoeing, swimming at the pool, hiking and wildlife viewing.

Buescher State Park

Activities at Buescher State Park include non-motorized boating, fishing in a stocked lake, nature study, hiking and biking. The 12-mile paved road between Buescher and Bastrop State Parks, which is winding and hilly, is ideal for biking.



McKinney Roughs Nature Park

west of Bastrop with 18 miles of hiking trails crisscrossing the 1,100 ares of bountiful nature. With its pervasive curves winding through Bastrop County, guests enjoy boating, canoeing, and fishing. One of the best access points to the Colorado River is in Bastrop, at Fisherman's Park. In Smithville there is access under the Texas 95 Bridge.

Birding at 9E

Birding is a favorite activity at the 9E Ranch Blue birds, Painted Buntings and Wild Turkey are often seen at the 9E Ranch. Bluebird houses are located throughtout pastures on the ranch. Birders come to the ranch and assist us in doing bird counts. Winter birds: Wood Duck, Ring-Necked Dove, Great Blue Heron, Black Vulture, Turkey Vulture, Killdeer, White-Winged Dove,Mourning Dove, Red-Bellied Woodpecker, Yellow-Bellied Sapsucker, Downy Woodpecker, Pileated Woodpecker, Crested Caracara, Eastern Phoebe, Blue Jay, American Crow, Carolina Chickadee , Tufted Titmouse, Black-Crested Titmouse, Carolina Wren, Ruby-Crowned Kinglet, Eastern Bluebird, Hermit Thrush, Northern Mockingbird, Cedar Waxwing, Pine Warbler, Yellow Rumped Warbler, Spotted Towhee, Chipping Sparrow, White-Throated Sparrow, White-Crowned Sparrow, Nothern Cardinal, Red-Winged Blackbird, Brown-Headed Cowbird, Pine Siskin, American Goldfinch Summer Counts added: Mourning Dove, Barred Owl, Chuck-Will's-Widow, Ruby-Throated Hummigbird, Scissor-Tailed Flycatcher,Pine Warbler, Summer Tanager, Painted Bunting, House Finch Blue-Gray Gnatcatcher



The Quiet Garden

The 9E Ranch has been a part of the Quiet Garden movement for over a decade providing outdoor space for the inner journey. The Quiet Garden Movement was started in England and now includes over 300 gardens worldwide. The idea is to provide access to outdoor space for prayer and reflection to create opportunities for people to experience silence, restfulness and contemplation. The Quiet Garden Movement flows from the example of Jesus' withdrawal to places to pray and his invitation to "Come with me by yourselves to a quiet place and get some rest " (Mark 6:31) The Gardens at the Ranch House on the 9E Ranch are open from 10:00 until dark for guests and we consider the entire ranch to be a Quiet Garden with many paths. Quiet Garden Retreat reduced rate with 3 night minimum in Daisy and Eagles' Nest Cabins on Monday, through Thursday nights. Just note Quiet Garden when you book.



Joan Bohls' Art

Joan Bohls has been painting in oils for over 2 decades and studied art/painting at The University of Texas as well as taking workshops from many well known artist. She is a member ofOil Painters of America, Outdoor Painter Society and Lost Pines Art CenterLPAC ( she serves on the Board of Directors ) and teaches Plein Air Painting.
